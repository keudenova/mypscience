🍽️ Mission 1: Nutrient Squad

20 XP available

☆☆☆

Every meal is really a delivery truck for seven different nutrient types. Each one has a different job — miss too much of one, and your body can't do that job properly.

🍞 Carbohydrates碳水化合物
Main energy source. Found in bread, rice, pasta, potatoes.
🥩 Proteins蛋白質
Growth and repair of cells. Found in meat, fish, beans, eggs.
🧈 Fats脂肪
Concentrated energy store, insulation. Found in oil, butter, nuts.
🍊 Vitamins維生素
Needed in tiny amounts for specific jobs — e.g. Vitamin C for healthy skin.
🧂 Minerals礦物質
e.g. calcium for bones, iron for blood.
🌾 Fibre纖維
Not digested — keeps food moving through the gut.
💧 Water水
No energy, but essential for every reaction in the body.

Every food is a mixture of nutrients — the three big "macronutrients" provide energy, the rest are needed in smaller amounts.

A missing nutrient causes a deficiency. Not enough Vitamin C → scurvy (bleeding gums). Not enough iron → anaemia (tiredness, weakness).

🔑 Mission 2: Enzyme Factory 酵素

20 XP available

☆☆☆

Large food molecules are too big to pass into your blood. Enzymes are biological catalysts — they speed up the breakdown of these molecules without being used up themselves.

The lock-and-key model: an enzyme's active site is a specific shape that only fits one matching substrate — like a key fitting one lock.

Because the active site shape only matches one substrate, each enzyme is specific — amylase can't digest protein, and protease can't digest starch.
Key vocabulary

Substrate — the molecule the enzyme acts on.
Active site — the pocket on the enzyme where the substrate fits.
Enzyme-substrate complex — the temporary joined structure formed during the reaction.
Products — the smaller molecules formed after the substrate is broken down.

🌡️ Mission 3: Conditions Lab

20 XP available

☆☆☆

Enzymes don't work equally well everywhere. Two conditions matter most: temperature and pH.

Rate rises with temperature, peaks at the optimum, then crashes as the enzyme denatures (its shape is destroyed).

Each enzyme has its own optimum pH — pepsin (stomach) works best around pH 2; amylase (mouth) prefers neutral pH 7.

Why denaturing happens

Heat or extreme pH breaks the bonds holding the enzyme's 3D shape together. The active site changes shape, the substrate no longer fits, and the enzyme stops working — permanently. This is different from just "slowing down"; a denatured enzyme won't recover even if conditions go back to normal.

Substrate concentration matters too: more substrate = faster rate, but only up to the point where every active site is already occupied ("saturated") — after that, adding more substrate makes no difference.

🚇 Mission 4: The Digestive Tunnel 消化系統

20 XP available

☆☆☆

Food travels through one long tunnel from mouth to exit, picking up help from three "accessory organs" along the way.

Food flows straight down the main tunnel (dark boxes). The liver, gallbladder and pancreas (gold boxes) don't touch the food directly — they inject bile and enzymes into the small intestine.

StomachChurns food; produces acid (kills bacteria, activates pepsin) and pepsin (digests protein)
LiverProduces bile (emulsifies fat — not an enzyme)

PancreasProduces amylase, protease and lipase, released into the small intestine

Three enzyme squads handle the three macronutrients. Each one breaks a big molecule into small, absorbable pieces.

Memory trick: Amylase → starch (both start with a vowel-ish sound), Protease → protein, Lipase → lipid (fat). The enzyme name usually matches its substrate.

🥗 Mission 6: Balanced Diet 均衡飲食

20 XP available

☆☆☆

A balanced diet means eating the right nutrients, in the right proportions, for your age, activity level and health. Too much or too little of any nutrient group causes problems.

Roughly: ● fruit & veg, ● starchy carbs, ● protein, ● dairy + a small amount of fats/sugars — proportions, not exact grams.

Too little...Can cause
ProteinPoor growth, muscle wasting
Vitamin CScurvy (bleeding gums)
IronAnaemia (tiredness)
CalciumWeak bones
Too much energy intake (more Calories than you use) is stored as fat — leading to obesity over time. Balance isn't just about variety, it's about amount too.

🏷️ Mission 7: Label Detective

20 XP available

☆☆☆

Every packaged food has a nutrition label — and you can calculate its energy yourself, without trusting the printed number.

Energy = (C × 4) + (P × 4) + (F × 9)
C — carbohydrate, grams
P — protein, grams
F — fat, grams
Answer in kcal
Worked example — using the label above

Step 1: Carbohydrate: 30 g × 4 = 120 kcal
Step 2: Protein: 5 g × 4 = 20 kcal
Step 3: Fat: 10 g × 9 = 90 kcal
Step 4: Total = 120 + 20 + 90 = 230 kcal per 100 g

Notice fat provides more than double the energy per gram compared to carbs or protein (9 vs 4) — that's why high-fat foods are so energy-dense.

🧬 Mission 8: Absorption Zone 吸收

20 XP available

☆☆☆

Digested food is now tiny — but it still has to cross into the blood. The small intestine wall is specially built for exactly this job.

Millions of finger-like villi line the small intestine, each packed with capillaries to carry nutrients away.

Three adaptations make the small intestine excellent at absorption:

AdaptationWhy it helps
Millions of villiHuge increase in surface area for absorption
Very thin walls (one cell thick)Short diffusion distance — nutrients cross quickly
Rich blood supplyKeeps carrying absorbed nutrients away, maintaining a concentration gradient
